ABOUT

Life Story

There's no short tl;dr here — the path from materials engineering to creative coding is too much fun to squeeze into a line or two.

I grew up in Bangladesh and got hooked on tech and gaming early. That curiosity led me to BUET, where I studied Materials and Metallurgical Engineering. There I realised I could build things without breaking them — well, mostly — by writing code. I started with small scripts and buggy experiments, then fell into how games work and ended up making a few of my own.

After my bachelor's I moved to Germany. I'm now doing a Master’s in Materials Science and Engineering at RWTH Aachen. On the side I build indie games with ZeezBit Studios, work on AI and automation in industry, and do creative coding and data viz whenever I get the chance. I like sitting at the intersection of materials and software: both are about understanding systems and making things that work.

When I'm not in front of a screen, I'm often looking up at the sky — I'm a stargazing noob, but I love it. And I still aspire to build products that reach a global audience and to bridge materials engineering and software in a way that's useful and real. If the chance comes, I'll keep making games too.

I aspire to create something real and useful that reaches a global user base, while bridging materials engineering and software.

Here’s to hoping it all works out.
